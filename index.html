My Interests

The concept surrounding Information Technology is new to me. My skills and knowledge regarding the process and procedures behind computational operations has yet to improve. What sparks my interest and curiosity in this field is how people can create products and services seemingly impossible before, such as self-driving cars, 3D printing, or AI. At the start of year 2020 was when my interest in IT started. In Cambodia, the use of emerging technology is at the beginning stages. There was no event that sparked my interest, however, driving around Phnom Penh, the capital of Cambodia, I began to envision how technology can be used to solve the everyday problems within the city. Another reason for my interest in IT is that there will most likely be an increase demand for jobs in the future for computer science and IT specialists, making it a lucrative job. In addition, the growth in the technology sector and the information technology sector will likely be exponential for years to come. Due to ITs worldwide recognition, businesses and organizations are always looking for a way to improve their operations therefore there is high job security and I won’t have to worry about a lack of IT related jobs.

Why I chose RMIT

RMIT is a top 1% of university in the world and a place of high-quality education where I can learn from the best Computer Science and Information Technology experts. It is an ideal place for further education in this field as it is recognized for innovation in technology and design, with a strong focus on emerging trends. In addition, the course structure favours my learning outcome as it allows me to explore the different streams of IT before directing my focus towards a specific major. Furthermore, job skill workshops and networking sessions provided by RMIT are a great way for me to develop both technical and soft skills to prepare for industry-related work. To add on, there is a large student body at RMIT therefore I feel like it is essential for my growth to engage and learn with people with different cultures, backgrounds, and experiences. Ultimately, these are my reasons for choosing RMIT.

Since we are currently in the era of the Fourth Industrial Revolution, innovative technology is constantly emerging, I hope to learn the hard skills and soft skills to successfully identify and solve twenty-first-century problems with modern solutions to improve communities and enrich people’s lives.
